About Unix Timestamps

Unix timestamp (also called Epoch time or POSIX time) is the number of seconds since January 1, 1970 at 00:00:00 UTC. It's used for:

  • Recording precise moments in time
  • Timestamping events in databases
  • API logging and monitoring
  • Cross-platform time tracking
  • System logging and debugging
